The Z bearing block mounts the MGN12 linear rail carriage to the gantry and carries the Z belt attachment points. The front idler parts redirect the A and B drive belts around the front corners of the gantry. Both assemblies required redesign for the 4040 frame and the MGN12 rail upgrade.

## MGN12 upgrade

Stock Voron 2.4 R2 uses **MGN9** linear rails on the Z axis. Voron Tower upgrades these to **MGN12** rails — a wider rail with a larger carriage block. MGN12 provides a larger bearing contact area, higher load capacity, and more resistance to moment loads compared to MGN9.

<Info>
  MGN12 rails are a drop-in replacement in terms of mounting hole spacing on the extrusion. The printed bearing block is what changes — the carriage interface pattern is different between MGN9 and MGN12.
</Info>

## Z bearing block

### Consolidation from three parts to one

In the stock Voron 2.4 R2, the Z bearing block assembly consists of three separate printed parts:

- `z_bearing_block` (main body)
- `z_bearing_block_top`
- `z_bearing_block_bottom`

In Voron Tower, these three parts are **merged into a single unified block**: `z_bearing_block_v8.stl`.

Consolidating the parts eliminates alignment issues between the top and bottom halves, reduces the number of fasteners needed to assemble the block, and makes the overall assembly stiffer — there is no longer a seam between the top and bottom sections that can flex under belt tension.

| File | Qty | Description |
|---|---|---|
| `z_bearing_block_v8.stl` | 4 | Unified Z bearing block (one per Z rail carriage) |
| `z_belt_clamp.stl` | 4 | Belt clamp that locks the Z belt end into the bearing block |

### Z belt clamp

The `z_belt_clamp.stl` holds the looped end of the Z belt against the bearing block body. It is retained by two M3 screws and cinches the belt in place so it cannot slip under gantry load. Print 4 — one per corner.

## Front idler

The front idlers redirect the CoreXY A and B drive belts around the front-left and front-right corners of the gantry. Each front idler is split into a top and bottom piece for printability.

| File | Qty | Position |
|---|---|---|
| `front_idler_top_a.stl` | 1 | Front-left idler, upper body |
| `front_idler_top_b.stl` | 1 | Front-right idler, upper body |
| `front_idler_bottom_a_1.stl` | 1 | Front-left idler, lower body |
| `front_idler_bottom_b_1.stl` | 1 | Front-right idler, lower body |

<Note>
  The `_1` suffix on the bottom parts indicates a single-piece lower body. Earlier design revisions split the bottom into multiple pieces; the current version consolidates to one piece per side.
</Note>

The top and bottom halves of each front idler bolt together around the idler pulley stack. The assembled idler then mounts to the front 4040 gantry extrusion using T-nuts.

## Assembly notes

<Steps>
  <Step title="Prepare the bearing block">
    Insert the MGN12 carriage interface screws (M3 × 8 SHCS) before mounting the block to the carriage. The screw holes are accessible from the back of the block.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Route and clamp the Z belt">
    Loop the Z belt end through the slot in the bearing block body, then capture it with `z_belt_clamp.stl`. Tighten the two M3 clamp screws evenly to avoid canting the clamp.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Assemble the front idler">
    Stack the idler pulleys (2× 625 bearings with a washer between them) and sandwich them between `front_idler_top_a/b` and `front_idler_bottom_a_1/b_1`. Secure with the through-bolt before mounting to the gantry.
  </Step>
</Steps>
