---

This guide covers assembly of all parts that differ from the stock Voron 2.4 R2. For toolhead, electronics, wiring, and XY gantry carriage assembly, follow the [official Voron 2.4 R2 assembly manual](https://docs.vorondesign.com/build/mechanical/) alongside this guide.

<Info>
  A full build walkthrough video is available on YouTube: [Voron Tower Build Video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=f0kk1qKtl04). Watch it alongside this guide for visual reference on each assembly stage.
</Info>

<Warning>
  This is a community modification. The 350×350mm build is the recommended and most tested configuration. The 500×500mm build retains the stock 2020 Z carriage and is less tested — proceed with additional caution if building at that scale.
</Warning>

## Before you begin

- Download and print all modified STL files from the [parts overview](/parts/overview).
- Review the [hardware list](/build/hardware-list) and confirm all parts are on hand.
- Read the [modifications overview](/build/modifications) to understand how Voron Tower differs from stock before starting.
- Use the Voron 2.4 R2 manual for all sections not covered here.

## Assembly

<Steps>

  <Step title="Frame assembly">
    The 4040 frame is assembled using the same general approach as the Voron 2.4 R2, but all structural members are 4040 series 10 extrusion.

    1. Lay out all vertical and horizontal extrusions and verify lengths against the parameter table output.
    2. Use M5 T-nuts (10mm slot) and M5×10 or M5×12 socket head screws throughout — standard 2020 M5 T-nuts will not fit.
    3. Assemble the four vertical corner posts with the bottom horizontal extrusions first, then add the top frame.
    4. Use a machinist square or the extrusion itself to confirm 90° joints before tightening. The extra mass of 4040 makes shimming easier but also harder to correct once tightened.
    5. Install all internal cross-braces and mid-frame extrusions per the Fusion 360 model.

    <Note>
      Gantry extrusions must be cut to lengths ending in 0 or 5 mm. Do not cut to arbitrary lengths — the parametric model assumes rounded values. Safe adjustment is ±3mm from calculated values for `gantry_x_front`, `gantry_x_back`, and `gantry_y`.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Z motor mount installation">
    Parts used: `z_motor_mount_a`, `z_motor_mount_b`

    1. Install heat-set inserts into all M3 boss holes on both motor mounts.
    2. Mount the Z motors (NEMA17) onto the printed mounts using M3×16 screws.
    3. Align each motor mount against the bottom frame extrusion. The 4040 bolt pattern positions the mount differently from the 2020 variant — verify against the CAD model.
    4. Use M5 roll-in T-nuts in the 4040 extrusion slot and M5×10 screws to secure each mount.
    5. Repeat for all four corners.

    <Warning>
      Do not overtighten the T-nut screws before tramming — you will need to adjust motor mount positions during belt tensioning.
    </Warning>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Z belt driver installation">
    Parts used: `z_belt_driver_a_1`, `z_belt_driver_a_2`, `z_belt_driver_b_1`, `z_belt_driver_b_2`, `belt_tensioner_a`, `belt_tensioner_b`

    1. Press the GT2 20T pulley onto each Z motor shaft. The pulley face should be flush with the shaft end.
    2. Assemble each two-piece belt driver (`_1` and `_2`) around the pulley, ensuring the belt path is clear and the tensioner pivot is oriented upward.
    3. Install the belt tensioner arm (`belt_tensioner_a` or `belt_tensioner_b`) with an M3×20 screw through the pivot boss. Use an M3 hex nut in the retention slot.
    4. Route the Z GT2 belt through the driver assembly. Leave belt length adjustment for after the Z idlers are installed.
    5. Repeat for all four Z drive corners, matching `_a` and `_b` parts to their respective sides.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Z idler installation">
    Parts used: `z_idler_4040_1_a`, `z_idler_4040_1_b`, `z_idler_4040_2_a`, `z_idler_4040_2_b`

    The Z idlers mount in the top corners of the 4040 frame and redirect the Z belt from vertical to horizontal. There are two idler variants (`_1` and `_2`) for different corner positions — each printer uses one of each per side.

    1. Install heat-set inserts into each idler body.
    2. Press a 625 bearing into the idler housing. Use a flat surface to press bearings in squarely.
    3. Secure the bearing with an M5×16 screw and M5 hex nut through the idler body.
    4. Slide M5 T-nuts into the top extrusion slots at each corner, then position the assembled idler and fasten with M5×10 screws. Match the correct variant to its corner: `z_idler_4040_1_a/b` for the front corners, `z_idler_4040_2_a/b` for the rear corners.
    5. Route the Z belt from the bottom drive, up the corner, over the idler pulley, and down to the bed carriage. Leave final tensioning until the bed is installed.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Z bearing block installation">
    Parts used: `z_bearing_block_v8`, `z_belt_clamp`

    <Note>
      The `z_bearing_block_v8` replaces three separate stock Voron 2.4 parts with a single unified block. Install heat-set inserts into the block body before attaching to the rail carriage.
    </Note>

    1. Install heat-set inserts into all M3 boss holes on `z_bearing_block_v8`.
    2. Attach the bearing block to the MGN12H carriage on the Z rail using M3×8 screws.
    3. Route the Z belt through the belt channel in the bearing block and clamp it with `z_belt_clamp` using two M3×12 screws.
    4. With the belt clamped, apply even tension by adjusting the motor mount position at the bottom, then tighten the T-nuts.
    5. Repeat for both Z rails.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Front idler installation">
    Parts used: `front_idler_top_a`, `front_idler_top_b`, `front_idler_bottom_a_1`, `front_idler_bottom_b_1`

    The front idlers redirect the XY A/B drive belts and mount differently from stock due to the 4040 extrusion geometry.

    1. Install heat-set inserts into the top and bottom idler bodies.
    2. Press smooth F695 flanged bearings into the top idler stacks, and toothed GT2 idlers into the lower idler bodies.
    3. Assemble the top idler onto the front gantry extrusion first, using M5 T-nuts and M5×10 screws. The `_a` and `_b` variants are mirrored — verify left/right orientation before fastening.
    4. Attach the bottom idler below the top idler on the same extrusion. Ensure the belt path is collinear between upper and lower idler when viewed from the front.
    5. Do not fully tighten until the A/B belts are routed.
  </Step>

  <Step title="A/B drive installation">
    Parts used: `a_drive_1`, `a_drive_2`, `b_drive_1`, `b_drive_2`

    1. Install heat-set inserts into both drive bodies.
    2. Mount the A and B NEMA17 motors onto their respective two-piece drive assemblies.
    3. Press GT2 20T pulleys onto the motor shafts.
    4. Secure each assembled drive to the rear gantry extrusion using M5 T-nuts and M5×12 screws. The drive position sets belt geometry — align carefully using the CAD model.
    5. Route A/B belts from the drive pulleys, forward to the front idlers, and down to the toolhead X carriage.
    6. Apply tension by sliding each drive rearward, then tighten the mounting screws.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Z chain lower mount installation">
    Parts used: `z_chain_lower_mount`

    1. Install heat-set inserts into the mount body.
    2. Attach the mount to the left side panel extrusion using M5 T-nuts and M5×10 screws, at the height specified in the CAD model.
    3. Connect the lower end of the Z drag chain to the mount. The chain should have enough slack to travel the full Z range without tension.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Panel clip installation">
    Parts used: `corner_clip_4040`, `midspan_clip_4040`

    Panel clips secure the enclosure panels to the 4040 frame. They are designed for the 10mm slot profile.

    1. Slide M3 roll-in T-nuts into the outer-facing slots of all frame extrusions that will hold panels.
    2. Snap `corner_clip_4040` pieces onto the corner joints first — one per visible corner edge per panel.
    3. Install `midspan_clip_4040` pieces along the middle of each panel edge. Spacing depends on panel rigidity; start with one clip per 150–200mm of edge length.
    4. Insert each panel (e.g., 3mm acrylic or polycarbonate) by angling it into the clips, then pressing the outer lip over the panel edge.
    5. Fasten each clip with M3×8 button-head screws into the T-nuts.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Door assembly">
    Parts used: `door_hinge_4040`, `door_handle_a`, `door_handle_b`, `door_latch_magnet_4040`

    1. Install heat-set inserts into the hinge bodies and latch body.
    2. Attach `door_hinge_4040` hinges to the left-side door panel extrusion using M5 T-nuts. Two hinges per door panel is standard.
    3. Mount the door panel onto the hinges and verify it swings freely without binding.
    4. Assemble the door handle from `door_handle_a` and `door_handle_b`. The two-piece design clamps around a door panel edge — fasten with M3×16 screws.
    5. Install `door_latch_magnet_4040` on the door and its matching counterpart on the frame. Insert 6×3mm magnets (press fit) into both latch halves. Verify the door closes flush and holds securely.

    <Tip>
      If the door does not close flush, adjust the hinge T-nut position slightly inward or outward before fully tightening.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

</Steps>

## Continuing with stock Voron 2.4 R2 steps

Once the modified components are installed, continue with the standard Voron 2.4 R2 manual for:

- Toolhead and extruder assembly
- Electronics mounting and wiring
- Bed leveling and traming
- Software setup (Klipper, Mainsail/Fluidd)

<Note>
  The Voron community maintains an excellent set of resources at [docs.vorondesign.com](https://docs.vorondesign.com) for all post-mechanical steps.
</Note>
